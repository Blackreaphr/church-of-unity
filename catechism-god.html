Overview

God is the fullness of being, wholly good, and the giver of every created good. God transcends the world and remains present to it as the cause of its existence. Human names for God arise from real encounters with divine action and from careful reasoning about being, goodness, truth, and love.

Language about God is analogical. Words such as Father, Wisdom, Word, and Spirit direct attention to one divine life that exceeds every image while meeting us through history and prayer. Adoration grows as understanding deepens and as life conforms to the good.

Philosophical Reflection

Classical reflection calls God the act of being itself, simple and without parts. In God there is no composition or change, since all perfection of being is present as a single fullness. Creatures participate in that goodness in finite ways. To speak truly of God is to affirm the source on whom all things depend and toward whom every intellect and heart is ordered.

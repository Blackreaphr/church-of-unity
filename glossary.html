Core Doctrinal Terms

Trinity: One God in three persons, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. One being with real personal distinction. Anchor: Matthew 28:19; 2 Corinthians 13:14.

Incarnation: The Word became flesh in Jesus Christ, true God and true human. Anchor: John 1:14; Philippians 2:6-8.

Grace: God's free gift that heals and elevates us, enabling our response. Anchor: Ephesians 2:8-9.

Justification: Being set right with God through Christ, by grace received in faith that works through love. Anchor: Romans 5:1; Galatians 5:6.

Sanctification: Growth in holiness by grace and cooperation. Anchor: 2 Corinthians 3:18; 1 Thessalonians 4:3.

Sin: Turning from God in thought, word, deed, or omission, which harms self and neighbor. Anchor: Romans 3:23.

Image of God (Imago Dei): Human dignity and vocation rooted in being created in God's image. Anchor: Genesis 1:27.

Free Will: Real human capacity to choose the good; grace makes freedom truer. Anchor: Deuteronomy 30:19; Galatians 5:13.

Predestination: God's purpose to bring people to life in Christ, held with real human freedom and responsibility by many Christians. Anchor: Romans 8:29-30; Ephesians 1:5.

Eschatology: Teaching on last things: death, judgment, heaven, hell, and the hope of new creation. Anchor: Revelation 21-22.

Natural Law: Moral wisdom knowable in part by reason and clarified by revelation. Anchor: Romans 2:14-15.

Philosophical Terms

Act and Potency: What is actual and what can be; a way to explain change and causality.

Final Cause: The end or purpose for the sake of which something is. Helps clarify vocation.

Person: A someone, not a something. Classical theology speaks of an individual substance of rational nature.

Nature (Essence): What a thing is and its stable capacities and ends.

Analogy: Words about God are true yet not exhaustive; neither identical to nor wholly unlike our words about creatures.

Theodicy: Reasoned account of how a good God relates to evil and suffering; best held with humility.

Practical and Spiritual Terms

Conscience: The inner judgment of reason about right and wrong. It should be formed and followed. Anchor: Romans 2:15.

Vocation: God's call to love in a state of life and in daily work and service.

Mercy: Love that meets need and heals guilt. Anchor: Luke 6:36.

Justice: Giving each their due and seeking right relation with God and neighbor. Anchor: Micah 6:8.

Stewardship: Faithful care of time, creation, gifts, and money. Anchor: Genesis 2:15; 1 Peter 4:10.

Sabbath: Rest in God that orders work and worship. Anchor: Exodus 20:8-11; Mark 2:27.

Lectio Divina: Prayerful reading of Scripture in steps: read, meditate, pray, rest, act.

Examen: A brief daily review of gratitude, fault, and next step in grace.

Scripture and Literary Terms

Covenant: God's binding promise that forms a people and finds fulfillment in Christ. Anchor: Genesis 12; Jeremiah 31:31.

Kingdom of God: God's reign of truth, justice, and peace made present in Christ. Anchor: Mark 1:15.

Parable: A short story that reveals truth and invites decision. Anchor: Luke 15.

Beatitude: A blessing that sketches the character of a disciple. Anchor: Matthew 5:3-12.

Origin

A community devoted to unity through study, prayer, honest dialogue, and service. We learn from Catholic and wider Christian sources and welcome philosophical conversation that serves truth and charity.

Commitments

  • Humility in teaching and learning
  • Charity in speech and disagreement
  • Clarity about sources and interpretations

Community Norms

  • Speak truthfully. Listen carefully. Do not condemn persons.
  • When needs are legal, medical, or mental health related, seek qualified help.
  • Use sources responsibly and cite them when possible.

Mission and Focus

The Church of Unity seeks truthful unity. We receive wisdom from Catholic and wider Christian tradition and ask careful questions with a philosophical spirit. We study, pray, and act with charity. Our focus is clear teaching, simple practices, and dialogue that serves the common good.

How We Learn

  • Philosophy: begin with basic questions and thoughtful methods that sharpen judgment and steady action.
  • Practice: use simple tools for prayer and reflection that form habits of love.
  • Sermons: read short reflections that connect Scripture to daily life.
  • Texts and Library: explore sources and key terms for further study.
  • Guides: print one‑page helps for lectio divina and examen.
  • Inquiry: pose questions and learn in conversation with others.

Intellectual Honesty and Hospitality

We state what is known, what is likely, and what remains open. We welcome questions and seek to give reasons for hope without pressure or manipulation.

Overview

Death teaches the gravity of love and the truth that time is precious. Grief honors the bond that remains. Christian hope rests in the resurrection of Jesus Christ and in the promise that God will set creation right.

Hope does not erase sorrow. Hope orders sorrow. It makes courage possible and sustains the work of mercy. To die well is to forgive, to ask forgiveness, and to entrust oneself to God.

Philosophical Reflection

Mortality clarifies the meaning of action. Ends matter. A life aimed at truth and love acquires a shape that can endure loss and yet not collapse into despair. Hope draws strength from promise rather than from prediction. It is rational where promise is trustworthy.

Overview

This page gathers a concise in-depth presentation of the Ten Commandments and offers philosophical notes on their meaning. It also names practices that Scripture forbids and gives counsel for discernment of claims about visions and prophecy.

The Commandments in Depth

  • 1. No other gods: Worship belongs to God alone. Philosophical note: the highest good is one. Idolatry disorders desire and action.
    Exod. 20.3
  • 2. The Name: Speak of God with truth and reverence. Philosophical note: language aims at reality; perjury corrodes trust.
    Exod. 20.7
  • 3. The Day: Keep holy rest. Philosophical note: work finds meaning within worship and mercy; rest orders time to the good.
    Exod. 20.8; Deut. 5.12
  • 4. Father and mother: Honor parents and rightful authority. Philosophical note: gratitude and justice support social peace.
    Exod. 20.12
  • 5. Life: Do not murder. Philosophical note: human life bears a good that claims protection and care.
    Exod. 20.13
  • 6. Marriage: Do not commit adultery. Philosophical note: promises bind persons in truth; fidelity heals and builds.
    Exod. 20.14
  • 7. Goods: Do not steal. Philosophical note: stewardship and justice order property to the common good.
    Exod. 20.15
  • 8. Truth: Do not bear false witness. Philosophical note: truth is a common good; lies wound the innocent.
    Exod. 20.16
  • 9–10. Desire: Do not covet what is another’s. Philosophical note: gratitude trains desire and clears envy.
    Exod. 20.17

Practices Scripture Forbids

Scripture warns against attempts to control hidden powers or to consult the dead. The Church of Unity follows these teachings for the sake of truth, freedom, and spiritual safety.

There shall not be found among you any one that maketh his son or his daughter to pass through the fire, or that useth divination, or an observer of times, or an enchanter, or a witch, or a charmer, or a consulter with familiar spirits, or a wizard, or a necromancer. For all that do these things are an abomination unto the LORD.
Deut. 18.10-12 (KJV)
Regard not them that have familiar spirits, neither seek after wizards, to be defiled by them: I am the LORD your God.
Lev. 19.31 (KJV)
And when they shall say unto you, Seek unto them that have familiar spirits, and unto wizards that peep... should not a people seek unto their God? for the living to the dead?
Isa. 8.19 (KJV)

Counsel: Avoid magic, summoning rituals, and attempts to speak with the dead. Seek the Lord in prayer and in the ordinary means of grace.

Claims and Discernment

Christians test every claim. The Church of Unity urges prudence toward persons who demand authority from private revelations or from claims about seeing heaven or hell. True teaching serves Scripture, charity, and humility.

Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God.
1 John 4.1 (KJV)
For there shall arise false Christs, and false prophets, and shall shew great signs and wonders; insomuch that, if it were possible, they shall deceive the very elect.
Matt. 24.24 (KJV)
But though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel unto you... let him be accursed.
Gal. 1.8 (KJV)

Counsel: Test by Scripture, fruits, and the mind of the Church. Prefer quiet holiness to spectacular claims.

Obedience to God

Christians obey God above all. Civil authority deserves respect in what is just. When a human command violates divine law, God must be obeyed first.

We ought to obey God rather than men.
Acts 5.29 (KJV)
Let every soul be subject unto the higher powers. For there is no power but of God... for rulers are not a terror to good works, but to the evil.
Rom. 13.1-3 (KJV)

Scripture quotations are from the King James Version.

Practice

Choose one commandment this week. Name the good it protects in your life. Make one concrete act that builds that good. Ask the Lord for prudence to refuse what harms truth, mercy, or worship.

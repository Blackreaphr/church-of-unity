A Brief Statement of Unity

God is one. All that exists participates in the good that flows from God.

Heaven is friendship with God, begun now through grace. Hell is the refusal of that friendship.

The moral law is revealed by God and written within human hearts, yet it reaches us through human language and history. The Ten Commandments are received as covenantal gifts that teach us how to love God and neighbor.

God is known by many names. These names describe real aspects of the one divine life. When we speak of “arms,” we speak about missions and attributes.

Human freedom is real. Providence sustains creation without erasing our agency. Our choices shape character now and bear weight in eternity.

Chaos and order are both present in the world. Wisdom seeks their right relation through study, prayer, and action.

Death is a passage. Life’s meaning is clarified by how we love, repent, and hope.

The Church of Unity learns from Catholic and Christian sources and welcomes philosophical conversation that serves truth and charity.

The Church of Unity stands at the meeting point of faith and philosophy. We receive the wisdom of Catholic and Christian tradition and seek its heart with care. Our aim is truthful unity, a clear acknowledgment of the divine that invites understanding and shared life.

The Interplay of Chaos and Order

Reality unfolds with structure and surprise. Order shapes. Chaos quickens. Each lends meaning to the other. Unity looks for the rhythm between them through patience, courage, and discernment.

Faith Without Ornament

Ritual carries power when it is understood. We choose a simple path of reflection, study, and action. Faith matures as thought, word, and deed come together. In that meeting, the divine becomes present within both structure and fracture.

A Community of Inquiry

We gather as seekers and partners in inquiry. The Church of Unity welcomes careful questions and shared learning. We examine Scripture, philosophy, and lived experience with equal seriousness. Our dialogue continues, our texts remain open, and our practice grows through humility and charity.

An Open Invitation

We invite all who wish to face uncertainty with clarity and hope. Stand within the tension of chaos and order, faith and reason, past and future. Unity honors difference and cultivates harmony where difference is recognized and held together.

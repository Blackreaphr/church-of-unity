The Ten in Brief

  • 1. No other gods: worship God alone; reject idols of power, pleasure, and self.
  • 2. The Name: speak of God with truth; refuse perjury, curse, and empty vows.
  • 3. The Day: keep holy rest; order work, worship, and delight toward God.
  • 4. Father and mother: honor parents and lawful authority; protect the weak.
  • 5. Life: do not murder; receive the good of life; resist hatred and vengeance.
  • 6. Marriage: do not commit adultery; keep fidelity; guard the heart and body.
  • 7. Goods: do not steal; practice stewardship, generosity, and justice.
  • 8. Truth: do not bear false witness; speak truth in charity; refuse gossip.
  • 9-10. Desire: do not covet what is another's; train desire through gratitude and contentment.

From Letter to Spirit

Jesus does not abolish the Commandments; he brings them to fullness. The Sermon on the Mount reads the law in the key of the heart (Matt. 5-7). Reverence for God becomes worship in spirit and truth. Rest becomes a sabbath that heals and serves. The prohibitions shelter goods that love seeks to build: truthful speech, fidelity, justice, and mercy.

Practice

Read Exodus 20 or Deuteronomy 5 slowly. Choose one commandment. Ask which good it protects in your life today. Name one concrete act that honors that good this week. At day's end, thank God for one step and ask mercy where you fell short.

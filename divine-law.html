Revelation and Human Authorship

The Church of Unity receives the Decalogue as revelation expressed through human language and preserved in living tradition. Many scholars study how Exodus 20 and Deuteronomy 5 were composed and edited; we honor that work while reading as Scripture.

4Honor father and motherRemember the SabbathRemember the Sabbath
5You shall not killHonor father and motherHonor father and mother
6You shall not commit adulteryYou shall not killYou shall not murder
7You shall not stealYou shall not commit adulteryYou shall not commit adultery
8You shall not bear false witnessYou shall not stealYou shall not steal
9You shall not covet your neighbor’s wifeYou shall not bear false witnessYou shall not bear false witness
10You shall not covet your neighbor’s goodsYou shall not covetYou shall not covet

Note: Catholic and Lutheran traditions count images within the first commandment and separate coveting into two. Orthodox and Reformed traditions separate images as the second and combine coveting as the tenth.

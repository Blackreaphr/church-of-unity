Overview

Humans bear the image of God. Intelligence, freedom, and the capacity for love mark this image in creaturely form. The calling of humanity is to love God and neighbor, to seek justice and mercy, and to cultivate the earth with wisdom.

Work belongs within a rhythm of worship and rest. Technical power matures when directed by virtue. Stewardship rejects exploitation and receives authority as service for the common good.

Philosophical Reflection

The image of God includes a call to virtue. Freedom takes shape as the stable power to choose the good. The moral life is not a private achievement. It grows as persons enter communities that teach truth, practice justice, and extend mercy. The more the will cleaves to the good, the more it becomes truly free.

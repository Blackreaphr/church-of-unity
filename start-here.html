One-Page Summary

God is one and the source of being. Creation is good and ordered toward communion. Human vocation is to love God and neighbor in freedom. Grace heals and elevates freedom. Death is a passage; hope rests in friendship with God begun now and fulfilled beyond death.

Catechism Topics

  • Creation
  • Human Vocation
  • Grace and Freedom
  • Death and Hope

God

One God, source of being, known through Scripture and tradition by many real attributes such as Wisdom, Justice, and Mercy. Heaven and hell are states of communion or separation from God.

Creation

Creation is good and intelligible. Many scholars speak of Scripture using ancient cosmology while teaching theological truth about God as creator.

Human Vocation

To seek unity with God and neighbor through truth and charity. The moral law forms conscience and community.

Grace and Freedom

Providence sustains creation. Human freedom has real dignity and consequence. Christian thinkers propose models that hold both together.

Death and Hope

Death clarifies meaning. Hope rests in God who calls us into friendship now and beyond death.

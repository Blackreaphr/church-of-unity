Welcome

We gather as seekers. Ask one clear question, offer sources when you can, and answer others with patience. Our aim is understanding in common.

Posting Guide

  • One question, one thread: Use a title that states the claim or topic.
  • Opener length: 100 words or fewer. Add sources or longer notes in a follow-up comment.
  • Clarity: Define key terms at first use. Example: providence, free will, natural law.
  • Charity: State the strongest version of another view before you critique it.
  • Sources: Cite Scripture by book, chapter, and verse. Name other sources by author and work.
  • Tone: Ask, do not accuse. Address ideas, not persons.
  • Boundaries: No legal, medical, or mental-health advice. Seek professional help when needed.

Starter Prompts

  • Is divine foreknowledge compatible with real human freedom?
  • How does natural law speak to today’s moral debates?
  • What practices help Christians walk through grief with hope?
  • Which images help worship, and which distract?

Inquiries in Focus

Format

  • What the question is asking
  • Main views presented
  • Two helpful sources to read next
  • One practice to try this week

Discernment Cues

Use these steps when you test a claim.

  • State the claim in one sentence.
  • Read Scripture that bears on the claim. Note where texts agree and where emphasis differs.
  • Consult tradition by checking trusted witnesses such as Augustine, Aquinas, Julian of Norwich, or a catechism.
  • Reason about the claim. Look for clear terms, valid inferences, and real-world coherence.
  • Consider fruits. Ask what the claim produces in life: faith, hope, and love, or their opposites.
  • Revise your view if needed and name what remains open.

Safety and Care

Some topics touch grief, trauma, and conscience. Share carefully and honor privacy. If you find yourself in distress, speak with a pastor, counselor, or trusted friend. You are not alone.

Moderation and Conduct

  • First reminder for tone.
  • Second reminder with a request to revise.
  • If a pattern continues, the thread closes.
